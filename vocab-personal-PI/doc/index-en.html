Abstract

The purpose of this vocabulary is to describe in a semantic manner information related to university Research Staff (PI).

Introduction back to ToC

This vocabulary represents information related to university Research Staff (PI). The data used in it comes from a set of public spanish universities, more specifically, they belong to: the Automonous University of Madrid (UAM), the Carlos III University of Madrid (UC3M), the Complutense University of Madrid (UCM), the King Juan Carlos University (URJC), the University of Valladolid (UVa) and the University of Huelva (UHU). In this way, this vocabulary describes information about the PI, which works in a university, belonging to a responsible unit as well as a specific knowledge area.

Moreover, it uses the geo and geosparql vocabularies to represent the geographical location of the university where the PI carries out his work, according to different coordinate reference systems commonly used for this type of data. Additionally, it reuses terms from the schema.org vocabulary for the representation of concepts and relations that are used to describe people. Finally, it also reuses the organizations vocabulary to represent the university entity, which gathers all the Research Staff (PI) that work in it.

Vocabulary for the representation of university Research Staff (PI): Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the classes and properties of the vocabulary that represents the data of university Research Staff (PI):

As mentioned before, the scope of this vocabulary is the representation of data from university Research Staff (PI). To accomplish this purpose, as it can be seen in the figure, several classes and properties have been defined in order to describe these data, such as the PersonalInvestigador class, which collects information about his date of birth, his year of incorporation, the year in which his contract ends and the duration in months of this contract. Furthermore, the PersonalInvestigador class has several SKOS concept schemes to represent his gender, his dedication, his organization type, the research staff type, the doctorate degree and the research staff mobility. Besides, it is described for each PI his nationality country (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), continent and aggregation of countries. Regarding the other classes, the PI works in a university being part of a specific knowledge area during a given year, as well as he also belongs to a responsible unit, for example, a department. Moreover, if the PI studied a doctorate degree, information about the doctorate university is also collected, such as the nationality of the country (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), continent and aggregation of countries where the university is located in. Additionally, it has been reused the vocabulary schema:Person to describe the PI. Finally, it has been also reused the vocabulary of org:Organization to show how the classes Universidad and UniversidadDoctorado (in case it exists) have a geographical location, represented by the vocabulary geosparql.
